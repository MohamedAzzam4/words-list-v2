# Unit 9: Freizeit — German Phrases

## 1. Core Everyday Phrases

### P01
**German:** Das Essen ist lecker.
**Meaning:** The food is delicious.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** das Essen, lecker
**Note:** A very common way to compliment food.

### P02
**German:** Was gibt es zum Frühstück?
**Meaning:** What is there for breakfast?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** das Frühstück
**Note:** Useful daily question at home or at a hotel.

### P03
**German:** Das Mittagessen ist fertig.
**Meaning:** Lunch is ready.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** das Mittagessen
**Note:** Common phrase used at home or in a canteen.

### P04
**German:** Wir essen das Abendessen um sieben.
**Meaning:** We eat dinner at seven.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** essen, das Abendessen
**Note:** Simple way to state dinner time.

### P05
**German:** Ich nehme ein Brötchen mit Butter.
**Meaning:** I'll have a bread roll with butter.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** nehmen, das Brötchen, die Butter
**Note:** Very typical breakfast order in Germany.

### P06
**German:** Möchtest du Brot mit Käse oder Wurst?
**Meaning:** Would you like bread with cheese or sausage?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** das Brot, der Käse, die Wurst
**Note:** Classic German breakfast question.

### P07
**German:** Ich esse gern Fleisch und Reis.
**Meaning:** I like to eat meat and rice.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** essen, das Fleisch, der Reis
**Note:** Use "gern" after the verb to express preference.

### P08
**German:** Der Fisch ist sehr frisch.
**Meaning:** The fish is very fresh.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** der Fisch, frisch
**Note:** Useful when shopping at a market or eating out.

### P09
**German:** Ich möchte zwei Eier zum Frühstück.
**Meaning:** I would like two eggs for breakfast.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** das Ei, das Frühstück
**Note:** "Eier" is the plural of "Ei."

### P10
**German:** Die Kartoffeln schmecken gut.
**Meaning:** The potatoes taste good.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** die Kartoffel, schmecken
**Note:** "Schmecken" is the key verb for describing taste.

### P11
**German:** Ich mag Nudeln mit Tomaten.
**Meaning:** I like pasta with tomatoes.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** mögen, die Nudel, die Tomate
**Note:** "Mögen" is used for general preferences.

### P12
**German:** Der Salat hat Zwiebeln.
**Meaning:** The salad has onions.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** der Salat, die Zwiebel

### P13

**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** essen, das Obst, das Gemüse

**Used words:** die Orange, süß, frisch

**Meaning:** Yes, that tastes very good!
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** schmecken
**Note:** Enthusiastic answer to "Schmeckt es dir?"

### P40
**German:** Nein danke, ich bin nicht hungrig.
**Meaning:** No thanks, I'm not hungry.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** hungrig
**Note:** Polite way to decline food.

### P41
**German:** Guten Appetit!
**Meaning:** Enjoy your meal!
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** Guten Appetit!
**Note:** Said before eating. Everyone at the table says it.

### P42

**Meaning:** Cheers!
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** Zum Wohl!
**Note:** Said when clinking glasses with drinks.

## 5. Informal / Slang Phrases

### S01
**German:** Das ist echt lecker!
**Meaning:** That's really delicious!
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** lecker
**Note:** "Echt" is casual for "really/truly."

### S02
**German:** Ich hab mega Hunger!
**Meaning:** I'm mega hungry!
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** hungrig
**Note:** "Mega" is common youth slang for "very/extremely."

### S03
**German:** Na ja, der Kuchen ist okay.
**Meaning:** Well, the cake is okay.
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** der Kuchen
**Note:** "Na ja" shows slight hesitation or mediocre opinion.

### S04
**German:** Keine Ahnung, was ich essen will.
**Meaning:** No idea what I want to eat.
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** essen
**Note:** Very common casual expression of indecision.

### S05
**German:** Alles klar, ich bringe die Getränke mit.
**Meaning:** All right, I'll bring the drinks.
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** mitbringen
**Note:** "Alles klar" means "all right / understood."

### S06
**German:** Der Kaffee ist voll gut!
**Meaning:** The coffee is totally good!
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** der Kaffee
**Note:** "Voll" is informal for "totally."

### S07
**German:** Passt schon, ich bin nicht durstig.
**Meaning:** It's fine, I'm not thirsty.
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** durstig
**Note:** "Passt schon" means "it's fine / no worries."

### S08
**German:** Die Schokolade ist echt süß!
**Meaning:** The chocolate is really sweet!
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** die Schokolade, süß
**Note:** "Echt" intensifies the adjective informally.
