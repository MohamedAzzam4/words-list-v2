# Unit 12: Restaurant — German Phrases

## 1. Core Everyday Phrases

### P01
**German:** Herzlichen Glückwunsch zum Geburtstag!
**Meaning:** Happy birthday! (Warm congratulations on your birthday!)
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** Herzlichen Glückwunsch!, der Geburtstag
**Note:** The most common birthday greeting in German.

### P02
**German:** Alles Gute zum Geburtstag!
**Meaning:** All the best for your birthday!
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** Alles Gute!, der Geburtstag
**Note:** Equally common birthday greeting, slightly more casual.

### P03
**German:** Wir feiern heute eine Party.
**Meaning:** We are celebrating a party today.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** feiern, die Party
**Note:** Simple announcement of a celebration.

### P04
**German:** Das Fest ist am Samstag.
**Meaning:** The celebration is on Saturday.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** das Fest
**Note:** "Fest" is more formal than "Party."

### P05
**German:** Ich schenke dir ein Geschenk.
**Meaning:** I'm giving you a gift.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** schenken, das Geschenk
**Note:** "Schenken" = to give (as a gift). Note the related noun "Geschenk."

### P06
**German:** Ich lade dich zur Party ein.
**Meaning:** I'm inviting you to the party.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** einladen, die Party
**Note:** Separable verb: "ein|laden."

### P07
**German:** Die Überraschung ist für dich!
**Meaning:** The surprise is for you!
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** die Überraschung
**Note:** Exciting phrase for gift-giving moments.

### P08
**German:** Ich gratuliere dir!
**Meaning:** I congratulate you! / Congrats!
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** gratulieren
**Note:** Used for birthdays, achievements, and good news.

### P09
**German:** Die Musik ist laut.
**Meaning:** The music is loud.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** die Musik
**Note:** Common observation at a party.

### P10
**German:** Ich schreibe eine Karte.
**Meaning:** I'm writing a card.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** die Karte
**Note:** "Karte" can mean card (greeting card, postcard) or ticket.

### P11
**German:** Die Kerzen sind auf dem Kuchen.
**Meaning:** The candles are on the cake.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** die Kerze
**Note:** Classic birthday scene. "Kerzen" is the plural.

### P12
**German:** Frohe Weihnachten!
**Meaning:** Merry Christmas!
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** Frohe Weihnachten!
**Note:** Standard Christmas greeting. Memorize as a fixed phrase.

### P13
**German:** Frohes neues Jahr!
**Meaning:** Happy New Year!
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** Frohes neues Jahr!
**Note:** Said on or around New Year's Day.

### P14
**German:** Die Blumen sind sehr schön.
**Meaning:** The flowers are very beautiful.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** die Blume
**Note:** Common compliment when receiving or seeing flowers.

### P15
**German:** Wir dekorieren das Zimmer.
**Meaning:** We're decorating the room.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** dekorieren
**Note:** Party preparation activity.

## 2. Questions You Can Ask

### P16
**German:** Wann ist dein Geburtstag?
**Meaning:** When is your birthday?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** der Geburtstag
**Note:** Very common getting-to-know-you question.

### P17
**German:** Wer kommt zur Party?
**Meaning:** Who is coming to the party?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** die Party
**Note:** Useful when planning a celebration.

### P18
**German:** Was schenkst du ihm?
**Meaning:** What are you giving him (as a gift)?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** schenken
**Note:** Discussing gift ideas.

### P19
**German:** Ist das Fest groß oder klein?
**Meaning:** Is the celebration big or small?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** das Fest
**Note:** Asking about the size of an event.

### P20
**German:** Hast du eine Karte geschrieben?
**Meaning:** Did you write a card?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** die Karte
**Note:** Past tense with "haben + Partizip" — simple enough for A1.

### P21

**Meaning:** How many candles does the cake have?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** die Kerze
**Note:** Fun birthday question.

### P22

**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral

### P23

**Level:** A1

### P25

**Used words:** die Überraschung

**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** die Blume
**Note:** Grateful response when receiving flowers.

### P27

## 5. Informal / Slang Phrases

### S01
**German:** Mega Party gestern!
**Meaning:** Awesome party yesterday!
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** die Party
**Note:** "Mega" is youth slang for "awesome/great."

### S02
**German:** Echt? Das ist für mich?
**Meaning:** Really? That's for me?
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** (general celebration context)
**Note:** Surprised informal reaction to receiving a gift.

### S03
**German:** Voll schön dekoriert!
**Meaning:** Totally nicely decorated!
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** dekorieren
**Note:** "Voll" = totally (informal intensifier).

### S04
**German:** Na ja, letztes Jahr war besser.
**Meaning:** Well, last year was better.
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** letztes Jahr
**Note:** "Na ja" shows mild disappointment or comparison.

### S05
**German:** Alles klar, ich bringe die Geschenke.
**Meaning:** All right, I'll bring the gifts.
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** das Geschenk
**Note:** "Alles klar" = understood / all right.

### S06
**German:** Keine Ahnung, was ich schenken soll.
**Meaning:** No idea what I should give as a gift.
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** schenken
**Note:** Very common pre-birthday dilemma expressed casually.
