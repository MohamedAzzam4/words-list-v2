# Unit 18: Health & Body — German Phrases

## 1. Core Everyday Phrases
### P01
**German:** Ich bin krank.
**Meaning:** I am sick.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** krank
**Note:** The most basic way to say you're unwell.

### P02
**German:** Ich bin müde.
**Meaning:** I am tired.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** müde
**Note:** Very common daily expression.

### P03
**German:** Ich fühle mich nicht gut.
**Meaning:** I don't feel well.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** sich fühlen
**Note:** Reflexive verb — essential for doctor visits.

### P04
**German:** Ich habe Kopfschmerzen.
**Meaning:** I have a headache.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** die Kopfschmerzen
**Note:** One of the most common health complaints.

### P05
**German:** Ich habe Bauchschmerzen.
**Meaning:** I have a stomachache.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** die Bauchschmerzen
**Note:** Another very common health complaint.

### P06
**German:** Mein Rücken tut weh.
**Meaning:** My back hurts.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** der Rücken, wehtun
**Note:** "Wehtun" is separable — "tut weh."

### P07
**German:** Ich habe Fieber.
**Meaning:** I have a fever.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** das Fieber
**Note:** Important phrase for describing symptoms.

### P08
**German:** Ich habe Husten und Schnupfen.
**Meaning:** I have a cough and a runny nose.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** der Husten, der Schnupfen
**Note:** Typical cold symptoms.

### P09
**German:** Ich habe eine Erkältung.
**Meaning:** I have a cold.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** die Erkältung
**Note:** General term for the common cold.

### P10
**German:** Ich brauche ein Medikament.
**Meaning:** I need a medication.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** das Medikament
**Note:** Useful at the pharmacy or doctor's office.

### P11
**German:** Ich nehme eine Tablette.
**Meaning:** I'm taking a pill.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** die Tablette
**Note:** "Eine Tablette nehmen" is a fixed expression.

### P12
**German:** Ich brauche ein Rezept vom Arzt.
**Meaning:** I need a prescription from the doctor.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** das Rezept, der Arzt
**Note:** In Germany, many medications require a prescription.

### P13
**German:** Gute Besserung!
**Meaning:** Get well soon!
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** Gute Besserung!
**Note:** The standard thing to say to someone who is sick.

### P14
**German:** Gesundheit ist wichtig.
**Meaning:** Health is important.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** die Gesundheit
**Note:** Also said after someone sneezes: "Gesundheit!"

## 3. Natural Answers and Reactions
### P26
**German:** Ja, ich bin krank. Ich habe eine Erkältung.
**Meaning:** Yes, I'm sick. I have a cold.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** krank, die Erkältung
**Note:** Combining two common health expressions.

### P27
**German:** Nein, ich bin gesund.
**Meaning:** No, I am healthy.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** gesund
**Note:** Positive answer to health inquiry.

### P28
**German:** Ja, mein Bauch tut weh.
**Meaning:** Yes, my stomach hurts.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** der Bauch, wehtun
**Note:** Describing stomach pain.

### P29
**German:** Nein, ich fühle mich gut. Ich bin fit.
**Meaning:** No, I feel good. I'm fit.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** sich fühlen, fit
**Note:** Positive health statement.

### P30
**German:** Danke, mir geht es besser.
**Meaning:** Thank you, I'm feeling better.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** (general health context)
**Note:** Response to "Gute Besserung" or a health inquiry.

### P31
**German:** Ich war gestern beim Arzt.
**Meaning:** I was at the doctor yesterday.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** der Arzt
**Note:** Uses past tense "war" — allowed at A1 with "gestern."

### P32
**German:** Der Arzt hat mir ein Rezept gegeben.
**Meaning:** The doctor gave me a prescription.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** der Arzt, das Rezept
**Note:** Describing a doctor visit result.

### P33
**German:** Ich habe keine Schmerzen mehr.
**Meaning:** I have no more pain.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** der Schmerz
**Note:** Reporting recovery from pain.

## 5. Informal / Slang Phrases
### S01
**German:** Mir geht's echt nicht gut.
**Meaning:** I'm really not feeling well.
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** (echt)
**Note:** "Echt" intensifies — very common in spoken German.

### S02
**German:** Ich bin mega müde heute.
**Meaning:** I'm super tired today.
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** müde
**Note:** "Mega" is popular slang for "very/super."

### S03
**German:** Keine Ahnung, was ich habe.
**Meaning:** No idea what's wrong with me.
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** (general health context)
**Note:** "Keine Ahnung" — very common casual expression.

### S04
**German:** Na ja, ich fühle mich so lala.
**Meaning:** Well, I feel so-so.
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** sich fühlen
**Note:** "Na ja" + "so lala" — casual, non-committal.

### S05
**German:** Passt schon, mir geht's besser.
**Meaning:** It's fine, I'm feeling better.
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** (general health context)
**Note:** "Passt schon" plays down concern.

### S06
**German:** Alles klar, ich gehe zum Arzt.
**Meaning:** All right, I'm going to the doctor.
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** der Arzt
**Note:** "Alles klar" as a casual acknowledgement.

### S07
**German:** Voll die Erkältung!
**Meaning:** Such a bad cold!
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** die Erkältung
**Note:** "Voll die..." is colloquial emphasis.

### S08
**German:** Echt jetzt? Du bist schon wieder krank?
**Meaning:** Seriously? You're sick again?
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** krank
**Note:** Expresses surprise with "Echt jetzt?"
