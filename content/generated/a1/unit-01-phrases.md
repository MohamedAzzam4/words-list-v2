# Unit 1: Begrüßungen — German Phrases

## 1. Core Everyday Phrases
### P01
**German:** Hallo! Wie geht's?
**Meaning:** Hello! How are you? (informal)
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** Hallo!, Wie geht's?
**Note:** The most common casual greeting in German.

### P02
**German:** Guten Morgen! Wie geht es Ihnen?
**Meaning:** Good morning! How are you? (formal)
**Level:** A1
**Register:** formal
**Used words:** Guten Morgen!, Wie geht es Ihnen?
**Note:** Used in formal or professional situations, typically before noon.

### P03
**German:** Guten Tag! Ich bin Frau Müller.
**Meaning:** Good day! I am Mrs. Müller.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** formal
**Used words:** Guten Tag!, ich, sein (ist), die Frau
**Note:** A polite way to introduce yourself formally.

### P04
**German:** Danke, gut. Und Ihnen?
**Meaning:** Thanks, good. And you? (formal)
**Level:** A1
**Register:** formal
**Used words:** Danke, gut., Sie, gut
**Note:** Standard polite reply to "Wie geht es Ihnen?"

### P05
**German:** Guten Abend! Wie geht es Ihnen?
**Meaning:** Good evening! How are you? (formal)
**Level:** A1
**Register:** formal
**Used words:** Guten Abend!, Wie geht es Ihnen?
**Note:** Used from late afternoon onwards.

### P06
**German:** Auf Wiedersehen, Herr Schmidt!
**Meaning:** Goodbye, Mr. Schmidt!
**Level:** A1
**Register:** formal
**Used words:** Auf Wiedersehen!, der Herr
**Note:** Formal farewell, used with last names.

### P07
**German:** Tschüs! Gute Nacht!
**Meaning:** Bye! Good night!
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** Tschüs!, Gute Nacht!
**Note:** Casual farewell in the evening.

### P08
**German:** Es geht. Nicht so gut.
**Meaning:** It's okay. Not so good.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** Es geht., Nicht so gut.
**Note:** Honest but casual response when things are mediocre.

### P09
**German:** Mir geht es sehr gut, danke!
**Meaning:** I'm doing very well, thanks!
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** sehr, gut, Danke!
**Note:** Enthusiastic positive response.

### P10
**German:** Ich heiße Anna. Und du?
**Meaning:** My name is Anna. And you?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** ich, heißen, du
**Note:** The most natural way to introduce yourself informally.

### P11
**German:** Ich komme aus Deutschland.
**Meaning:** I come from Germany.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** ich, kommen
**Note:** Basic sentence for saying where you are from.

### P12
**German:** Wie heißen Sie?
**Meaning:** What is your name? (formal)
**Level:** A1
**Register:** formal
**Used words:** heißen, Sie
**Note:** Formal way to ask someone's name.

### P13
**German:** Mein Name ist Peter.
**Meaning:** My name is Peter.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** der Name, sein (ist)
**Note:** Slightly more formal alternative to "Ich heiße..."

### P14
**German:** Bitte, Entschuldigung!
**Meaning:** Please, excuse me!
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** Bitte!, Entschuldigung!
**Note:** Used to get attention politely or to apologize.

### P15
**German:** Danke sehr!
**Meaning:** Thank you very much!
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** Danke!, sehr
**Note:** A stronger form of thanks.

## 2. Questions You Can Ask
### P16
**German:** Wie heißt du?
**Meaning:** What's your name? (informal)
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** heißen, du
**Note:** Informal version — used with friends, peers, or children.

### P17
**German:** Wie geht es Ihnen heute?
**Meaning:** How are you today? (formal)
**Level:** A1
**Register:** formal
**Used words:** Wie geht es Ihnen?, gut
**Note:** Adding "heute" (today) makes it more personal.

### P18
**German:** Wie ist Ihr Name?
**Meaning:** What is your name? (formal)
**Level:** A1
**Register:** formal
**Used words:** sein (ist), der Name, Sie
**Note:** Very formal — used in offices or official situations.

### P19
**German:** Woher kommen Sie?
**Meaning:** Where do you come from? (formal)
**Level:** A1
**Register:** formal
**Used words:** kommen, Sie
**Note:** Standard question about origin in formal register.

### P20
**German:** Woher kommst du?
**Meaning:** Where do you come from? (informal)
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** kommen, du
**Note:** Casual version for peers and friends.

### P21
**German:** Geht es Ihnen gut?
**Meaning:** Are you doing well? (formal)
**Level:** A1
**Register:** formal
**Used words:** gehen (geht), Sie, gut
**Note:** A polite way to check on someone.

### P22
**German:** Ist das Frau Meier?
**Meaning:** Is that Mrs. Meier?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** sein (ist), die Frau
**Note:** Asking to confirm someone's identity.

### P23
**German:** Wer ist das?
**Meaning:** Who is that?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** sein (ist)
**Note:** Simple question to identify a person.

**Register:** neutral

## 5. Informal / Slang Phrases
### S01
**German:** Echt? Du heißt auch Anna?
**Meaning:** Really? Your name is also Anna?
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** du, heißen, auch
**Note:** "Echt?" is informal for "Really?"

### S02
**German:** Na ja, es geht.
**Meaning:** Well, it's okay.
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** Es geht.
**Note:** "Na ja" softens the response — very common in spoken German.

### S03
**German:** Alles klar! Tschüs!
**Meaning:** All clear! Bye!
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** Tschüs!
**Note:** "Alles klar" means "alright" or "got it" — very common casual expression.

### S04
**German:** Mega gut! Danke!
**Meaning:** Super good! Thanks!
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** gut, Danke!
**Note:** "Mega" is slang for "super" or "really" — popular among younger speakers.

### S05
**German:** Keine Ahnung, wie er heißt.
**Meaning:** No idea what his name is.
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** heißen
**Note:** "Keine Ahnung" = "no idea" — extremely common in everyday speech.

### S06
**German:** Passt schon! Danke!
**Meaning:** It's fine! Thanks!
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** Danke!
**Note:** "Passt schon" = "it's alright / no worries" — casual reassurance.

### S07
**German:** Voll nett! Danke sehr!
**Meaning:** Really nice! Thank you very much!
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** Danke!, sehr
**Note:** "Voll" is slang for "really" or "totally."

### S08
**German:** Hallo! Alles klar bei dir?
**Meaning:** Hello! Everything okay with you?
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** Hallo!, du
**Note:** Casual greeting asking how someone is doing.
