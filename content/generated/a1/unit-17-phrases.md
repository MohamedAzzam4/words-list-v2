# Unit 17: Plans, Wishes & Modal Verbs — German Phrases

## 1. Core Everyday Phrases
### P01
**German:** Ich habe einen Plan.
**Meaning:** I have a plan.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** der Plan
**Note:** Simple sentence to express having a plan.

### P02
**German:** Hast du einen Wunsch?
**Meaning:** Do you have a wish?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** der Wunsch
**Note:** Common way to ask someone what they'd like.

### P03
**German:** Mein Traum ist wichtig.
**Meaning:** My dream is important.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** der Traum, wichtig
**Note:** Expressing the value of a personal dream.

### P04
**German:** Die Zukunft ist wichtig.
**Meaning:** The future is important.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** die Zukunft, wichtig
**Note:** A reflective statement about the future.

### P05
**German:** Ich möchte gern Deutsch lernen.
**Meaning:** I would like to learn German.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** möchten, Ich möchte gern ...
**Note:** Classic A1 phrase using "möchte gern" to express a polite wish.

### P06
**German:** Ich werde bald fertig.
**Meaning:** I will be done soon.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** werden, bald
**Note:** Simple future statement using "werden" + "bald."

### P07
**German:** Wir planen eine Reise.
**Meaning:** We are planning a trip.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** planen
**Note:** Useful for talking about making plans.

### P08
**German:** Ich hoffe, das ist möglich.
**Meaning:** I hope that is possible.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** hoffen, möglich
**Note:** Combines hoping and possibility.

### P09
**German:** Ich wünsche dir alles Gute!
**Meaning:** I wish you all the best!
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** wünschen
**Note:** Very common well-wishing phrase in German.

### P10
**German:** Du musst jetzt gehen.
**Meaning:** You must go now.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** müssen
**Note:** Modal verb "müssen" to express necessity.

### P11
**German:** Was soll ich machen?
**Meaning:** What should I do?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** sollen
**Note:** Asking for advice or direction.

### P12
**German:** Darf ich hier sitzen?
**Meaning:** May I sit here?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** dürfen
**Note:** Polite permission request — very common in daily life.

## 5. Informal / Slang Phrases
### S01
**German:** Ich will das echt machen!
**Meaning:** I really want to do that!
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** (echt)
**Note:** "Echt" adds emphasis in spoken German.

### S02
**German:** Keine Ahnung, was ich später machen soll.
**Meaning:** No idea what I should do later.
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** sollen, später
**Note:** "Keine Ahnung" is a very common informal expression.

### S03
**German:** Na ja, planen wir das später.
**Meaning:** Well, let's plan that later.
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** planen, später
**Note:** "Na ja" softens statements casually.

### S04
**German:** Mega, das ist mein Traum!
**Meaning:** Awesome, that is my dream!
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** der Traum
**Note:** "Mega" expresses excitement, popular among younger speakers.

### S05
**German:** Passt schon, ich muss nicht kommen.
**Meaning:** It's fine, I don't have to come.
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** müssen
**Note:** "Passt schon" means "it's alright" in casual speech.

### S06
**German:** Alles klar, ich hoffe, das klappt!
**Meaning:** All clear, I hope that works out!
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** hoffen
**Note:** "Alles klar" is a universal informal acknowledgement.

### S07
**German:** Ich möchte gern, aber ich darf nicht.
**Meaning:** I'd like to, but I'm not allowed to.
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** möchten, dürfen, Ich möchte gern ...
**Note:** Expressing a conflict between wanting and being permitted.

### S08
**German:** Voll der gute Plan!
**Meaning:** Such a great plan!
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** der Plan
**Note:** "Voll" intensifies — very colloquial.
