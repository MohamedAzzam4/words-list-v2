# Unit 5: Essen & Trinken — German Phrases

## 1. Core Everyday Phrases
### P01
**German:** Wo ist mein Schlüssel?
**Meaning:** Where is my key?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** der Schlüssel
**Note:** Very common question when leaving the house.

### P02
**German:** Die Tasche ist schwer.
**Meaning:** The bag is heavy.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** die Tasche, schwer
**Note:** Useful for describing bags, luggage, or backpacks.

### P03
**German:** Mein Handy ist kaputt.
**Meaning:** My phone is broken.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** das Handy, kaputt
**Note:** Very frequent complaint in daily life.

### P04
**German:** Ich brauche meine Brille.
**Meaning:** I need my glasses.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** die Brille
**Note:** "Brille" is always singular in German, even though it refers to a pair.

### P05
**German:** Hast du einen Regenschirm?
**Meaning:** Do you have an umbrella?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** der Regenschirm
**Note:** Common question on rainy days.

### P06
**German:** Die Flasche ist leicht.
**Meaning:** The bottle is light.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** die Flasche, leicht
**Note:** "leicht" can also mean "easy" in other contexts.

### P07
**German:** Dieses Ding ist wichtig.
**Meaning:** This thing is important.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** das Ding, dieser, wichtig
**Note:** "dieser/diese/dieses" changes with gender. "Dieses" for das-words.

### P08
**German:** Ich habe kein Feuerzeug.
**Meaning:** I don't have a lighter.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** das Feuerzeug, kein
**Note:** "kein" negates nouns. Use "kein" for neuter (das) nouns.

### P09
**German:** Die Uhr ist schön.
**Meaning:** The watch is nice.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** die Uhr
**Note:** "Uhr" means both "watch" and "clock."

### P10
**German:** Wo ist mein Geldbeutel?
**Meaning:** Where is my wallet?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** der Geldbeutel
**Note:** Also called "Portemonnaie" in some regions.

## 2. Questions You Can Ask
### P11
**German:** Ist das dein Schlüssel?
**Meaning:** Is that your key?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** der Schlüssel
**Note:** Simple yes/no question with possessive "dein."

### P12
**German:** Hast du eine Kreditkarte?
**Meaning:** Do you have a credit card?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** die Kreditkarte
**Note:** Useful in shops and restaurants.

### P13
**German:** Ist diese Tasche schwer?
**Meaning:** Is this bag heavy?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** die Tasche, dieser, schwer
**Note:** "diese" is the feminine form of "dieser."

### P14
**German:** Was ist in der Dose?
**Meaning:** What is in the can?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** die Dose
**Note:** "Dose" can mean "can" or "tin."

### P15
**German:** Ist die Batterie leer?
**Meaning:** Is the battery empty?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** die Batterie
**Note:** "leer" (empty) pairs naturally with "Batterie."

### P16

**Meaning:** Who has my book?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** das Buch
**Note:** Simple question using "wer" (who).

### P17

**Meaning:** Is this product good?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** das Produkt, dieser
**Note:** Useful when shopping.

## 3. Natural Answers and Reactions
### P18
**German:** Nein, ich habe keinen Schlüssel.
**Meaning:** No, I don't have a key.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** der Schlüssel, kein
**Note:** "keinen" is the accusative masculine form of "kein."

### P19
**German:** Ja, die Tasche ist in der Küche.
**Meaning:** Yes, the bag is in the kitchen.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** die Tasche
**Note:** A natural answer to a location question.

### P20
**German:** Nein, das ist nicht meine Brille.
**Meaning:** No, that is not my glasses.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** die Brille
**Note:** "nicht" negates the sentence, "meine" shows feminine possession.

### P21
**German:** Das Foto ist sehr schön.
**Meaning:** The photo is very nice.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** das Foto
**Note:** A compliment you can give after seeing someone's photo.

### P22
**German:** Die Zeitung ist von heute.
**Meaning:** The newspaper is from today.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** die Zeitung
**Note:** "von heute" = from today.

### P23
**German:** Diese Seife riecht gut.
**Meaning:** This soap smells good.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** die Seife, dieser
**Note:** "riechen" (to smell) is a useful everyday verb.

**Used words:** die Uhr, kaputt

## 5. Informal / Slang Phrases
### S01
**German:** Mein Handy ist echt kaputt!
**Meaning:** My phone is really broken!
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** das Handy, kaputt
**Note:** "echt" adds emphasis, very common in spoken German.

### S02
**German:** Keine Ahnung, wo mein Schlüssel ist.
**Meaning:** No idea where my key is.
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** der Schlüssel
**Note:** "Keine Ahnung" is a very common spoken expression.

### S03
**German:** Alles klar, ich habe die Tasche.
**Meaning:** All clear, I have the bag.
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** die Tasche
**Note:** "Alles klar" = okay / all clear — used constantly.

### S04
**German:** Na ja, das Ding ist halt schwer.
**Meaning:** Well, the thing is just heavy.
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** das Ding, schwer
**Note:** "Na ja" = well; "halt" adds a casual tone.

### S05
**German:** Passt schon, ich brauche kein Foto.
**Meaning:** It's fine, I don't need a photo.
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** das Foto, kein
**Note:** "Passt schon" = it's fine / no worries.
