# Unit 21: Gefühle — German Phrases

## 1. Core Everyday Phrases
### P01
**German:** Man muss hier leise sein.
**Meaning:** You have to be quiet here.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** man, müssen, leise
**Note:** Common instruction in libraries, hospitals, etc.

### P02
**German:** Man darf hier nicht laut sprechen.
**Meaning:** You are not allowed to speak loudly here.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** man, dürfen, laut
**Note:** Uses the fixed phrase "Man darf hier nicht ..."

### P03
**German:** Das ist verboten.
**Meaning:** That is forbidden.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** verboten
**Note:** Very common expression to state something is not allowed.

### P04
**German:** Das ist hier erlaubt.
**Meaning:** That is allowed here.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** erlaubt
**Note:** Opposite of "verboten."

### P05
**German:** Ich brauche eine Erlaubnis.
**Meaning:** I need a permission.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** die Erlaubnis
**Note:** Useful when asking about official permissions.

### P06
**German:** Kannst du das Schild lesen?
**Meaning:** Can you read the sign?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** das Schild
**Note:** Pointing to a sign in public.

### P07
**German:** Man soll hier nicht rauchen.
**Meaning:** You should not smoke here.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** man, sollen
**Note:** "Sollen" expresses a recommendation or expectation.

### P08
**German:** Die Ordnung ist wichtig.
**Meaning:** Order is important.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** die Ordnung
**Note:** A very German cultural value!

### P09
**German:** Es gibt ein Verbot für Hunde.
**Meaning:** There is a ban on dogs.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** das Verbot
**Note:** Common in parks and shops.

### P10
**German:** Man muss die Regeln kennen.
**Meaning:** You have to know the rules.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** man, müssen, die Regel
**Note:** General statement about knowing rules.

## 5. Informal / Slang Phrases
### S01
**German:** Echt? Das ist verboten?
**Meaning:** Really? That is forbidden?
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** verboten
**Note:** "Echt?" is a casual way to express surprise.

### S02
**German:** Keine Ahnung, was die Regel ist.
**Meaning:** No idea what the rule is.
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** die Regel
**Note:** "Keine Ahnung" = very common informal phrase for "I don't know."

### S03
**German:** Na ja, man muss das machen.
**Meaning:** Well, you have to do it.
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** man, müssen
**Note:** "Na ja" softens the statement casually.

### S04
**German:** Alles klar, ich bin leise.
**Meaning:** All right, I'll be quiet.
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** leise
**Note:** "Alles klar" = informal acknowledgment.

### S05
**German:** Passt schon, ich darf das.
**Meaning:** It's fine, I'm allowed to do that.
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** dürfen
**Note:** "Passt schon" = casual "it's okay."
