# Unit 19: Appearance & Personality — German Phrases

## 1. Core Everyday Phrases
### P01
**German:** Sie hat blonde Haare.
**Meaning:** She has blonde hair.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** blond, das Haar
**Note:** Basic physical description.

### P02
**German:** Er hat dunkle Haare.
**Meaning:** He has dark hair.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** dunkel, das Haar
**Note:** "Dunkel" is used for hair, eyes, and colors.

### P03
**German:** Sie hat lange Haare.
**Meaning:** She has long hair.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** lang, das Haar
**Note:** Describing hair length.

### P04
**German:** Er hat kurze Haare.
**Meaning:** He has short hair.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** kurz, das Haar
**Note:** Opposite of "lang."

### P05
**German:** Er hat einen Bart.
**Meaning:** He has a beard.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** der Bart
**Note:** Simple description of facial hair.

### P06
**German:** Sie ist schlank.
**Meaning:** She is slim.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** schlank
**Note:** A neutral, polite way to describe body type.

### P07
**German:** Er ist sehr freundlich.
**Meaning:** He is very friendly.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** freundlich
**Note:** Common compliment about personality.

### P08
**German:** Sie ist sehr nett.
**Meaning:** She is very nice.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** nett
**Note:** One of the most common positive descriptions.

### P09
**German:** Er ist lustig.
**Meaning:** He is funny.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** lustig
**Note:** Describing someone's humor.

### P10
**German:** Sie sieht hübsch aus.
**Meaning:** She looks pretty.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** hübsch, aussehen
**Note:** "Aussehen" is separable — "sieht ... aus."

### P11
**German:** Er ist sehr intelligent.
**Meaning:** He is very intelligent.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** intelligent
**Note:** Complimenting someone's intellect.

### P12
**German:** Sie ist ruhig und freundlich.
**Meaning:** She is quiet and friendly.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** ruhig, freundlich
**Note:** Combining two personality traits with "und."

## 2. Questions You Can Ask
### P13
**German:** Wie sieht er aus?
**Meaning:** What does he look like?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** aussehen
**Note:** Essential question for physical descriptions.

### P14
**German:** Wie sieht sie aus?
**Meaning:** What does she look like?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** aussehen
**Note:** Same question, feminine form.

### P15
**German:** Hat er lange oder kurze Haare?
**Meaning:** Does he have long or short hair?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** lang, kurz, das Haar
**Note:** Using "oder" to offer alternatives.

### P16
**German:** Ist sie blond?
**Meaning:** Is she blonde?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** blond
**Note:** Simple yes/no question about hair color.

### P17
**German:** Ist er nett oder unfreundlich?
**Meaning:** Is he nice or unfriendly?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** nett, unfreundlich
**Note:** Asking about personality with contrasting options.

### P18
**German:** Wie ist sein Charakter?
**Meaning:** What is his character like?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** der Charakter
**Note:** Asking about someone's personality traits.

### P19
**German:** Wie ist ihr Gesicht?
**Meaning:** What is her face like?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** das Gesicht
**Note:** Asking about facial features.

### P20
**German:** Was trägt er heute?
**Meaning:** What is he wearing today?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** tragen
**Note:** "Tragen" for wearing clothes.

### P21
**German:** Ist sie lustig oder ruhig?
**Meaning:** Is she funny or quiet?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** lustig, ruhig
**Note:** Asking about personality with alternatives.

### P22
**German:** Ist er dünn oder dick?
**Meaning:** Is he thin or fat?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** dünn, dick
**Note:** Direct question — be careful in context, can be rude.

## 5. Informal / Slang Phrases
### S01
**German:** Der sieht echt gut aus!
**Meaning:** He really looks good!
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** aussehen
**Note:** "Echt" adds emphasis — very common in spoken German.

### S02
**German:** Sie ist mega nett!
**Meaning:** She is super nice!
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** nett
**Note:** "Mega" intensifies the compliment.

### S03
**German:** Keine Ahnung, wie er aussieht.
**Meaning:** No idea what he looks like.
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** aussehen
**Note:** "Keine Ahnung" — a very natural casual filler.

### S04
**German:** Voll der nette Typ!
**Meaning:** Such a nice guy!
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** nett
**Note:** "Voll der/die/das..." is colloquial emphasis.

### S05
**German:** Na ja, er ist halt ein bisschen faul.
**Meaning:** Well, he's just a bit lazy.
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** faul
**Note:** "Na ja" + "halt" — casual and forgiving.

### S06
**German:** Alles klar, sie ist echt sympathisch.
**Meaning:** All right, she's really likeable.
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** sympathisch
**Note:** "Alles klar" as a casual opener.

### S07
**German:** Er ist voll lustig!
**Meaning:** He is so funny!
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** lustig
**Note:** "Voll" as intensifier — youth slang.
