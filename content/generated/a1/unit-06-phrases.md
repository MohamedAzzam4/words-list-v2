# Unit 6: Kleidung — German Phrases

## 1. Core Everyday Phrases
### P01
**German:** Ich arbeite im Büro.
**Meaning:** I work in the office.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** das Büro
**Note:** "im" = in dem (in the). Very common phrase.

### P02
**German:** Der Computer funktioniert nicht.
**Meaning:** The computer is not working.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** der Computer, funktionieren
**Note:** A very frequent complaint at work.

### P03
**German:** Kann ich den Drucker benutzen?
**Meaning:** Can I use the printer?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** der Drucker
**Note:** Polite request with "kann ich."

### P04
**German:** Der Bildschirm ist groß.
**Meaning:** The screen is big.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** der Bildschirm
**Note:** "Bildschirm" literally means "picture screen."

### P05
**German:** Wo ist die Tastatur?
**Meaning:** Where is the keyboard?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** die Tastatur
**Note:** Simple location question.

### P06
**German:** Die Maus ist neu.
**Meaning:** The mouse is new.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** die Maus
**Note:** "Maus" means both animal mouse and computer mouse.

### P07
**German:** Ich telefoniere gerade.
**Meaning:** I'm on the phone right now.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** telefonieren
**Note:** "gerade" = right now. Very useful in office settings.

### P08
**German:** Hast du einen Kugelschreiber?
**Meaning:** Do you have a pen?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** der Kugelschreiber
**Note:** Often shortened to "Kuli" in spoken German.

### P09
**German:** Ich brauche einen Bleistift.
**Meaning:** I need a pencil.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** der Bleistift
**Note:** "Bleistift" literally means "lead pencil."

### P10
**German:** Ich habe kein Papier mehr.
**Meaning:** I don't have any paper left.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** das Papier
**Note:** "kein ... mehr" = no more.

### P11
**German:** Der Brief ist für dich.
**Meaning:** The letter is for you.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** der Brief
**Note:** "für dich" = for you (informal).

### P12
**German:** Ich brauche eine Briefmarke.
**Meaning:** I need a stamp.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** die Briefmarke
**Note:** Used for sending traditional letters.

**Level:** A1

**Used words:** der Drucker, drucken
**Note:** A classic office problem.

**Used words:** schreiben, der Brief

**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** der Kollege, telefonieren

**Meaning:** I'm sending the email now.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** schicken
**Note:** Describes an immediate action.

### P32
**German:** Bitte drucken Sie das Papier.
**Meaning:** Please print the paper.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** drucken, das Papier
**Note:** Formal "Sie" form, appropriate for workplace.

### P33
**German:** Ich öffne den Computer.
**Meaning:** I'm turning on the computer.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** öffnen, der Computer
**Note:** "öffnen" can mean "to open" or "to start up."

### P34
**German:** Ich schließe das Programm.
**Meaning:** I'm closing the program.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** schließen
**Note:** Common when working on a computer.

### P35

**Meaning:** The appointment is at ten o'clock.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** der Termin
**Note:** "um" + time = at (a specific time).

### P36

**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** die Besprechung

## 5. Informal / Slang Phrases
### S01
**German:** Der Computer ist echt langsam.
**Meaning:** The computer is really slow.
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** der Computer
**Note:** "echt" adds emphasis in casual speech.

### S02
**German:** Keine Ahnung, wie das Passwort ist.
**Meaning:** No idea what the password is.
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** das Passwort
**Note:** "Keine Ahnung" = no idea — very common spoken phrase.

### S03
**German:** Alles klar, ich drucke das.
**Meaning:** All right, I'll print that.
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** drucken
**Note:** "Alles klar" = all right / understood.

### S04
**German:** Na ja, das Internet ist halt langsam.
**Meaning:** Well, the internet is just slow.
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** das Internet
**Note:** "Na ja" and "halt" are casual filler words.

### S05
**German:** Passt schon, ich schicke die Datei.
**Meaning:** It's fine, I'll send the file.
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** schicken, die Datei
**Note:** "Passt schon" = it's fine / no worries.

### S06
**German:** Voll stressig heute im Büro!
**Meaning:** Really stressful today in the office!
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** das Büro
**Note:** "voll" = really/totally in casual speech.

### S07
**German:** Mega, der neue Bildschirm ist toll!
**Meaning:** Awesome, the new screen is great!
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** der Bildschirm
**Note:** "mega" = awesome/great in youth slang.

### S08
**German:** Echt, die Kollegin ist mega nett!
**Meaning:** Really, the colleague is super nice!
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** die Kollegin
**Note:** "echt" and "mega" are common casual intensifiers.
