# Unit 8: Tagesablauf — German Phrases

## 1. Core Everyday Phrases
### P01
**German:** Hast du Lust?
**Meaning:** Do you feel like it? / Are you up for it?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** Hast du Lust?
**Note:** Very common way to suggest an activity.

### P02
**German:** Ja, gern!
**Meaning:** Yes, gladly! / Sure!
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** Ja, gern!
**Note:** The most natural way to accept an invitation.

### P03
**German:** Leider nicht.
**Meaning:** Unfortunately not.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** Leider nicht.
**Note:** Polite way to decline. Softer than "nein."

### P04
**German:** Tut mir leid.
**Meaning:** I'm sorry.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** Tut mir leid.
**Note:** Used to apologize or express regret.

### P05
**German:** Gute Idee!
**Meaning:** Good idea!
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** Gute Idee!, die Idee
**Note:** Enthusiastic agreement to a suggestion.

### P06
**German:** Ich möchte ins Kino gehen.
**Meaning:** I would like to go to the cinema.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** möchten
**Note:** "möchten" = would like — very polite form.

### P07
**German:** Kannst du heute kommen?
**Meaning:** Can you come today?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** können, heute
**Note:** Simple question about availability.

### P08
**German:** Ich will morgen schwimmen gehen.
**Meaning:** I want to go swimming tomorrow.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** wollen, morgen
**Note:** "wollen" = to want. More direct than "möchten."

### P09
**German:** Wir treffen uns am Abend.
**Meaning:** We're meeting in the evening.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** treffen, der Abend, am
**Note:** "uns treffen" = to meet each other (reflexive).

### P10
**German:** Ich habe am Morgen Zeit.
**Meaning:** I have time in the morning.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** der Morgen, die Zeit, am
**Note:** "am Morgen" = in the morning.

### P11
**German:** Was machst du am Wochenende?
**Meaning:** What are you doing on the weekend?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** das Wochenende, am
**Note:** The most common weekend question.

### P12
**German:** Ich habe eine Verabredung.
**Meaning:** I have a date / an appointment.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** die Verabredung
**Note:** "Verabredung" is informal — for friends, dates. Not for doctor appointments.

## 2. Questions You Can Ask
### P13
**German:** Möchtest du mitkommen?
**Meaning:** Would you like to come along?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** möchten, mitkommen
**Note:** Very common invitation. "mit-kommen" = to come along.

### P14
**German:** Wann fängt der Film an?
**Meaning:** When does the movie start?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** anfangen
**Note:** "anfangen" is separable: "fängt ... an."

### P15
**German:** Wann hören wir auf?
**Meaning:** When do we stop?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** aufhören
**Note:** "aufhören" is separable: "hören ... auf."

### P16
**German:** Hast du heute Zeit?
**Meaning:** Do you have time today?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** heute, die Zeit
**Note:** Very natural way to check availability.

### P17
**German:** Können wir uns morgen treffen?
**Meaning:** Can we meet tomorrow?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** können, morgen, treffen
**Note:** "uns treffen" = to meet each other.

### P18
**German:** Was möchtest du am Abend machen?
**Meaning:** What would you like to do in the evening?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** möchten, der Abend, am
**Note:** Open-ended question for evening plans.

### P19
**German:** Willst du am Nachmittag kommen?
**Meaning:** Do you want to come in the afternoon?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** wollen, der Nachmittag, am
**Note:** "am Nachmittag" = in the afternoon.

### P20
**German:** Was machen wir dann?
**Meaning:** What do we do then?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** dann
**Note:** Asking about the next step in a plan.

### P21
**German:** Treffen wir uns um drei?
**Meaning:** Shall we meet at three?
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** treffen, um
**Note:** "um" + time = at (a specific time).

## 3. Natural Answers and Reactions
### P22
**German:** Ja, ich kann heute kommen.
**Meaning:** Yes, I can come today.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** können, heute
**Note:** Positive answer to an invitation.

### P23
**German:** Tut mir leid, ich kann nicht.
**Meaning:** I'm sorry, I can't.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** Tut mir leid., können
**Note:** Polite decline combining apology and inability.

### P24
**German:** Ja, gern! Gute Idee!
**Meaning:** Yes, gladly! Good idea!
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** Ja, gern!, Gute Idee!
**Note:** Enthusiastic double acceptance.

### P25
**German:** Leider nicht. Ich habe keine Zeit.
**Meaning:** Unfortunately not. I don't have time.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** Leider nicht., die Zeit
**Note:** Natural decline with a reason.

### P26
**German:** Vielleicht können wir morgen gehen.
**Meaning:** Maybe we can go tomorrow.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** vielleicht, können, morgen
**Note:** Suggesting an alternative.

### P27
**German:** Jetzt habe ich Zeit.
**Meaning:** I have time now.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** jetzt, die Zeit
**Note:** Confirming current availability.

### P28
**German:** Gestern war ich nicht da.
**Meaning:** Yesterday I wasn't there.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** gestern
**Note:** "war" = was (past tense of sein). Acceptable at A1.

### P29
**German:** Am Wochenende habe ich frei.
**Meaning:** On the weekend I'm free.
**Level:** A1
**Register:** neutral
**Used words:** das Wochenende, am
**Note:** "frei haben" = to be free / have time off.

## 5. Informal / Slang Phrases
### S01
**German:** Hast du Lust, morgen mitzukommen?
**Meaning:** Do you feel like coming along tomorrow?
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** Hast du Lust?, morgen, mitkommen
**Note:** Casual invitation combining key phrases.

### S02
**German:** Echt? Das ist mega cool!
**Meaning:** Really? That's super cool!
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** —
**Note:** "echt" and "mega" are very common slang intensifiers.

### S03
**German:** Keine Ahnung, was wir heute machen.
**Meaning:** No idea what we're doing today.
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** heute
**Note:** "Keine Ahnung" = no idea — very frequent in casual speech.

### S04
**German:** Na ja, vielleicht morgen dann.
**Meaning:** Well, maybe tomorrow then.
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** vielleicht, morgen, dann
**Note:** "Na ja" = well — casual filler. Laid-back response.

### S05
**German:** Passt schon, wir treffen uns jetzt.
**Meaning:** It's fine, we'll meet now.
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** treffen, jetzt
**Note:** "Passt schon" = it's fine / no worries.

### S06
**German:** Alles klar, ich komme am Abend.
**Meaning:** All right, I'll come in the evening.
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** der Abend, am
**Note:** "Alles klar" = all right / understood.

### S07
**German:** Voll die gute Idee!
**Meaning:** Totally good idea!
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** die Idee
**Note:** "voll die gute" is a casual intensifier pattern.

### S08
**German:** Wir machen das zusammen, passt schon!
**Meaning:** We'll do it together, no worries!
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** zusammen
**Note:** Reassuring and casual closing statement.

### S09
**German:** Ich will echt mitkommen!
**Meaning:** I really want to come along!
**Level:** A1/A2
**Register:** informal
**Used words:** wollen, mitkommen
**Note:** "echt" emphasizes the desire to join.
